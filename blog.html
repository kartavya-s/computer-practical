Simple Guide to Cyber Threats and How to Protect Your Computer

What is a Computer Virus?

A computer virus is a type of malicious software program ("malware") that, when executed, replicates itself by modifying other computer programs and inserting its own code. Viruses can damage your files, steal information, or slow down your system.

What is Malware?

Malware is short for "malicious software". It includes viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, and spyware. Malware is designed to harm your computer, steal personal data, or take control of your device without your permission.

What is Spam?

Spam refers to unwanted emails, often sent in bulk, usually for advertising. Some spam emails may also contain phishing links or malware. Always be careful when opening emails from unknown senders.

What is Antivirus Software?

Antivirus software is designed to detect, prevent, and remove malicious software. It scans your system for viruses and other threats, keeping your data and system safe.

Tips to Stay Safe Online:

  • Install antivirus software and keep it updated.
  • Don't open suspicious emails or download unknown files.
  • Keep your operating system and apps updated.
  • Use strong, unique passwords.
