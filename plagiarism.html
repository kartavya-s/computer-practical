What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism means copying someone else's work or ideas and saying they are your own. It can happen in school, writing, music, and more. Plagiarism is unfair and wrong.

Famous Cases of Plagiarism

  • Jayson Blair (USA): A reporter who copied stories. He lost his job.
  • Melania Trump (USA): Parts of her speech were copied. People criticized her.
  • Annette Schavan (Germany): She copied in her PhD work. Her degree was taken away.
  • Kaavya Viswanathan (USA): She copied parts of a book. Her book was removed.

Punishment in Different Countries

  • USA: Students can be suspended. Writers can be fined.
  • Germany: Degrees can be cancelled. Jobs can be lost.
  • India: Students may fail or be banned from publishing.
  • China: Students can be expelled. Teachers can lose titles.
  • UK: Students may fail exams or be expelled.
